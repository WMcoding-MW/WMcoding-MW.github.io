Subgroups and Center of a group April 16th, 2022 11:18 AM

Def: A subset H of a group (G,*) ex: H $\subseteq$ G is a subgroup if:

(identity) 1) $e_G \in H$

(closure) 2) If $h_1,h_2 \in H$, then $h_1 * h_2 \in H$.

(inverse) 3) If h$\in H$ then $h^{-1} \in H$.

If all three are true, we denote the subgroup as H$\leq$G.

  • Examples

    • 1) {e} $\leq$ G and G $\leq$ G. This is always true and these subgroups are called trivial subgroups of G.

    2) $\mathbb{Z} \leq \mathbb{R}$ (under addition)

    3) $l \in \mathbb{Z}, l\mathbb{Z}$:={$lm | m \in \mathbb{Z}$}$\subseteq \mathbb{Z}$ (also called the left cosets, we will see this later.)

    4) $SL_2(\mathbb{R}) = ${$A\in M_2(\mathbb{R}) | det(A)=1$}$\leq GL_2(\mathbb{R})$

Proposition: If $H_1 \leq G$, $H_2 \leq G$ are subgroups of G then K = $H_1 \cap H_2 \leq G$

Proof: 1) e $\in H_1$, e $\in H_2$ => e $\in H_1,H_2$. 2) $a,b \in K => a*b\in H_1,H_2$. 3) $a\in K => a^{-1} \in H_1,H_2$, thus $a^{-1} \in H_1 \cap H_2 = k$

Remark: Similarly the intersection of an arbitrary number of subgroups is a subgroup. ex: $2\mathbb{Z} \cap 3\mathbb{Z} = 6\mathbb{Z} \leq \mathbb{Z}$

  • Centralizer of a group

    • Def: Let (G,*) be a group and let g$\in G$. The centralizer of g in G is Ƶ :={$x \in G | x * g = g * x$}

    Claim: $\forall g\in G, Ƶ(g)$ is a subgroup of G.

    Proof: 1) e*g=g*e=g => e $\in Ƶ(g)$

    2) let $h_1,h_2 \in Ƶ(g)$, then $(h_1*h_2)*g = h_1 * (h_2 * g) $=> $h_1 * (g * h_2)$, thus $h_2 \in Ƶ(g)$, next $g * (h_1 * h_2) => h_1 \in Ƶ(g)$ Thus $h_1 * h_2 \in Ƶ(g)$

    3) $h \in Ƶ(g)$, want $h^{-1} * g = g * h^{-1}$ <=> $h*h^{-1}*g=h*g*h^{-1}$ <=> $g = h * g * h^{-1}$ <=> g*h=h*g.

    2) $Ƶ(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix})$

    $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix}$ = $\begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$
