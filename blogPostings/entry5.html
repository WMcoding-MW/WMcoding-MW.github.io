Intro to groups(Symmetric and Basic properties) April 13th, 2022 12:54 PM

Def: A pair (G, *) where G is a set and * is a binary operation on G,G is called a group if:

1) (Associativity) $\forall a,b,c \in G$ a * (b * c) = (a * b) * c

2) (identity element) $\exists e \in G : \forall a \in G$ a * e = e * a = a (=> G $\neq \emptyset$, since e$\in$G)

3) (inverse element) $\forall a \in G: \exists b \in G$ a * b = b * a = e

further more, a group (G, *) is called abelian if a*b = b*a. (also known as communative groups)

  • Exercises:

    • 1) ($\mathbb{Z}$, +) is a group with e = 0 and inverse of a is -a. (Verify)

    2) ($\mathbb{R}_{>0}$, x) is a group. Associative: a * (b * c) = (a * b) * c, since a,b,c>0

    Identity is 1, a * 1 = 1 * a = a. finally inverse: if a$\in\mathbb{R}_{>0}$ then $\frac{1}{a}\in\mathbb{R}_{>0}$, thus a * $\frac{1}{a}$ = $\frac{1}{a}$ * a = 1.

    Thus ($\mathbb{R}_{>0}$, x) is a group. (side note, avoid $\frac{1}{a}$, use $a^{-1}$ instead. it'll be important later.)

    Ex of a non abelian group: $GL_2(\mathbb{R})$:={$\begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \\ \end{bmatrix} : a,b,c,d\in\mathbb{R}$ and $ad-bc \neq 0$} (important group remember this!). (Not a group under multiplication as well)

    3)If (G, *) is a group, then (G, *') is also a group where a*'b = b*a $\forall a,b\in G$. (Verify!)

  • Important groups: permutations and symmetric groups

    • Let X be a non empty set, we define $S_X$ := {$\sigma: X\to X$ | $\sigma$ is a bijection}. Binary operations on $S_X$ are defined as $S_X$ x $S_X \to S_X$ s.t. ($\sigma$,e) = $\sigma$ o e.

    proof: Associativity is okay since composition of maps is Associative.

    Identity: x$|\to$x is the identity element.

    Inverse: Since $\sigma \in S_X$ is a bijiection, $\exists \sigma^{-1} \in S_X$ s.t. $\sigma$ o $\sigma^{-1}$ = $\sigma^{-1}$ o $\sigma = e$

    Def: ($S_X$, o) is called the symmetric group on X.

    Def: if X = {1,2,...,m}, where m$\in \mathbb{Z}_{>0}$, then $S_X$ = {1,2,...,m} is denoted by $S_m$ and called the symmetric group of degree m. (it's the group of permutations of m elements).

    Ex m=3: $\sigma \in S_3$ can be represented as a matrix of size 2 x m $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ \sigma(1) & \sigma(2) & \sigma(3) \end{bmatrix}$ where $\sigma(1), \sigma(2), \sigma(3)$ is a permutation of 123. for example, $\sigma = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 1 & 3 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$, $\phi = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 2 & 3 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$, then $\sigma$ o $\phi = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 3 & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$. Note $\sigma = \sigma^{-1}$ and $\phi^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 3 & 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$.

    IMPORTANT NOTATION: more compact version of matrix representation is known as cycle notation ex (12)$\in S_3, 1|\to 2|\to 1$ and 3 is fixed. (note (12) and (21) are equivalent.)
